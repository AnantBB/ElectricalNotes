           Let us consider new phenomenon that is associated with current electricity. Description that we have made so far is also a part of current electricity, but this phenomenon not only explains flow of charged particles but also magnetic field associated with flow of current. When current flows in straight conductor magnetic field is produced around the conductor in the form of concentric circles. When conductor is straight magnetic field is comparatively weak. But if we make the current to pass through circular conductor then the most of the field is concentrated within the circular path, through which current passes. So the strength of magnetic field for circular path is higher than straight conductor. Further we may wound more number of turns instead of a single turn; under such geometrical configuration, magnetic field strength is still higher in comparison to single turn.

           The same prototype is shown in above figure. When certain numbers of turns are wound, to increase the magnetic field strength, such device is known as solenoid, the figure that we are looking at, is a solenoid’s figure. The red rounds, of elliptical shape are, the paths followed by magnetic field when current passes through turns of conductor. We should keep in mind that elemental current carrying path is also sufficient to create the magnetic field. Forming a turn or a coil is only required to increase magnetic field strength.

           It is beneficial to put and understand the analogy between parameters of current electricity or ohms law, and magnetic field parameters. Voltage in current electricity is also called by a name emf that is analogous to mmf or magneto-motive force. Current is analogous to magnetic flux and lastly resistance is analogous to reluctance of magnetic path. Voltage is cause and current is effect, which corresponds to mmf is cause and magnetic field is effect. Further resistance and reluctance are both depend upon geometry of their shape in similar way, and both have specific value corresponding to materials used to carry current or magnetic flux.

           The first equation in figure 2 is similar to ohms law, that states as mmf = reluctance* flux. The mmf is a product of current and number of turns, and it is a cause of the magnetic field. mmf is measured in ampere-turns. The magnetic flux is measured in webers. The formula for reluctance of magnetic path is similar to resistance formula, except the constant used is permeability, so it is appearing in denominator. Another difference is all the magnetic flux is not confined to the space assigned to carry it, which is termed as leakage flux.

           If the current carried by solenoid coil is constant then flux established in magnetic path is also constant, and magnetic flux do not make any effect on solenoid’s performance except its existence. This feature is deliberately stated to start explaining the effect of change in magnetic flux on associated circuit. The change in magnetic flux, generates emf in the circuit, which is generally termed as induced emf. Therefore we have differentiated the equation NI = Φ*S with respect to time in Figure-2. The parameters Φ and I are only dependent on time and rest of them are constant. We have differentiated the equation and also multiplied by N. The result is that we could rearrange the equation to show the expression for induced emf. This induced emf is known as self-induced emf and parameter L is known as coefficient of self-induction. We should note that value of L is N2/S where both N and S are constants so L is also a constant.

           Now we will start building a solenoid coil which is known as inductor. The selected outer dimensions of solenoid or inductor are internal diameter is 30 mm, external diameter is 65 mm and axial length is 100 mm. This inductor is wound by using 1 mm2 wire and 1424 turns are wound to form an inductor. We want to find the resistance of the coil first, so value of mean diameter is calculated from internal and external diameters first. From mean diameter 47.5 mm we can find out length of mean turn which is π into mean diameter Dm. Now we know resistivity of copper that is 16.8 mili-ohms, conductor length would be length of mean turn into number of turns 1424 and cross section of conductor is 1 mm2. In this way resistance calculates to be 3.59 ohms.

           Next constant that we may calculate is the coefficient of self-inductance L. First we have to find out the value of S that is reluctance of magnetic path. For finding this value we know permeability of free space 1.2566e-6, axial length is 100 mm or 0.1 meters and cross section of magnetic circuit from internal diameter of coil which is 30 mm. Once we find the value of reluctance of magnetic circuit we can find out the second constant L from N2/S. This second constant calculates to 18 mH.

           Now we are equipped to understand how inductor will responds to application of voltage and responds to change in current through it. If we look at the inductor that we have built more closely, then, it is clear that it is not simply a wire resistance but something more than that. We may separate out the resistance from coil for analysis purpose. What remains after separating out resistance is known as pure inductor without resistance. In this way we have second element in our discussion that is inductor. Similar to resistance, inductor is also shown schematically, as it is shown in Figure-3.

           The circuit that we see have resistance of 3.59 ohms and inductor is of 18 mH. These two elements are connected in series and are supplied by 12 volts dc supply. Since we have represented the inductor coil in the form of elements in the circuit drawn at right top corner, we can approach the phenomenon systematically. But if we consider that coil is connected to 12 volts supply having 3.59 ohms of resistance then we may conclude from ohms law that current of 3.34 amperes shall flow. But this estimation is not completely true; the reason is we have assumed that circuit will come to steady state instantaneously. Under steady state, rate of change of current is zero so induced emf is zero so this calculation is correct.

           For understanding transient phenomenon we have to consider the starting instance at t = 0, before t = 0 current is zero, but instantaneously current could not reach steady state value, this implies di/dt is too large and voltage across inductor would be very high. For approaching the problem we have to write differential equation, using Kirchhoff’s voltage law. Corresponding differential equation is written beyond the circuit diagram. If we observe this differential equation, we see that first term corresponds to voltage drop across inductor, second term is voltage drop across resistor and sum of these two voltages are equated to dc voltage Vdc.

           The solution of differential equation has a constant steady state current term multiplied by exponential term. If we substitute t=0 in the solution then i = 0. Further as we substitute value of t corresponding to 1 to 5 time constants, value of current reaches steady state value exponentially. The reason about why initial value of current is zero, is found by looking at next equation for voltage across inductor, this value is equal to Vdc at t = 0. Note that in our inductor the value of time constant is 5 mili- seconds.

           In this way we have reached the point where we have current through inductor and voltage across inductor in the form of mathematical expression. Now let us move our attention towards graphical representation of these expressions. The first graph below yellow line shows raising current, with initial value of current is zero. But second graph, which is voltage across inductor is equal and opposite to applied voltage of 12 volts dc. This situation is indicating toward a thump rule: “inductor acts like a open circuited element at t = 0”.

           The next point that we should note is rate of change of current in first graph and rate of change of voltage in second graph are both maximum at t = 0. The value of current raised and value of voltage dropped in first time constant in both graphs is maximum, that is 63% of final value of current and 63% of initial value of voltage. In other words the phenomenon is fast in first time constant, and becomes slow in all subsequent times. When transient comes to practically steady state, after five time constants, rate of change of current with respects to time is almost zero. So voltage across inductor is zero, and presence of inductor gets nullified.

           Let us recall the start of explaining the inductor, we said another effect in addition to flow of current is the establishing of magnetic field. Now we could clearly distinguish between two phenomenon, after separating resistance from inductor coil. Inductor’s operation is entirely depends on magnetic field. The third graph below the yellow line is a graph of inductor power against time. The first two graphs are inductor voltage and inductor current, if we take their product then the result is the power supplied to inductor or power absorbed by inductor. The energy absorbed by inductor is stored in the form of magnetic field. Since flux and current are directly proportional, when current raises exponentially flux also raises exponentially.

           If we observe power against time graph then power raises to a peak value before completion of first time constant. After value of power reaches to maximum and drops before first time constant energy supplied by 12 volts dc supply is 40 m-Joules. We should note that energy is a quantity like fluid that is supplied in 5 mili-sec. The ratio of energy and time is power or rate at which energy is supplied to inductor. The average power for first time constant is 40/5 = 8 watts, which is quite close to peak power of 10 watts. In second time constant period more 35 mili-joules of energy is stored in the inductor in the form of magnetic field. The average power for this period is 7 watts which is comparatively low. For subsequent time periods the value of energy drops to 15, 6 and 3 mili-Joules respectively. Corresponding power values are 3, 1.5 and 0.75 watts, that supports our view that inductor no more function as inductor since rate of change of current is reaching towards zero.
